Malibu

Singular properties along the coast

Malibu stretches across more than twenty miles of coastline, from oceanfront on the sand to gated hillside and canyon estates. Land value often drives price, properties are highly individual, and second homes and investment holdings are common.

That individuality shapes financing at every turn. Appraisals require specialist attention, coastal location brings insurance and permitting considerations, and many buyers are structuring purchases as investments or additional residences rather than primary homes.

For investors, evaluating a property on its rental income can open options; for others, asset-based review reflects the fuller financial picture. The right path depends on how the home will be owned and used.

  • Unique and oceanfront property appraisal
  • Insurance and coastal considerations
  • Second-home and investment financing
  • DSCR / rental-income evaluation
  • Asset-based and portfolio review
  • Large, land-driven loan amounts
