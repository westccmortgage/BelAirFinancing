Jumbo Purchase Strategy

Financing for purchase prices well beyond conforming limits.

Most estate purchases in Los Angeles sit far above conforming loan limits, which places them in jumbo territory. At that level, the loan is less a product than a structure — shaped by your liquidity, your timeline, and how strong your offer needs to read.

Overview

What a jumbo strategy really involves

A jumbo loan is any mortgage that exceeds the conforming limit set for a given county. In the Los Angeles luxury market, that describes the majority of transactions. Because these loans are held or priced differently by lenders, they carry their own documentation expectations, reserve requirements, and appraisal scrutiny.

The right jumbo structure depends on more than the purchase price. Down payment, reserves after closing, how title will be held, and the balance between fixed and interest-only payments all shape what is realistic — and how competitive your offer looks to a seller.

  • The purchase price exceeds county conforming limits
  • You want your offer to read as strong and credible
  • Liquidity and reserves need to be positioned carefully
  • Both fixed and interest-only options are on the table
  • Title may be held in a trust or entity

How We Approach It

Matched to your situation, not a template

We start with the property and your position, not a rate sheet — the timeline, the competition for the home, and how your assets are held all come first.

From there we compare realistic options side by side: payment design, reserve requirements, and documentation path, so the trade-offs are clear before you commit.
