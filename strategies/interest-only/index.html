Interest-Only Mortgage Options

Payment structures that keep capital working elsewhere.

For many estate buyers, the goal is not to pay a home off as quickly as possible — it is to keep capital available for a business, a portfolio, or the next opportunity. Interest-only structures are built for exactly that.

Overview

How interest-only structures work

An interest-only mortgage lets you pay only the interest portion for an initial period, keeping the required monthly payment lower and freeing cash for other uses. After that period, payments adjust to include principal.

These structures suit borrowers with strong liquidity and variable income who prefer flexibility over forced amortization. They are a tool, not a default — the right fit depends on your cash-flow priorities and how long you expect to hold the property.

  • Cash flow matters more than fast principal paydown
  • Capital is better deployed in business or investments
  • Income is strong but variable or seasonal
  • You expect to hold the property for a defined period
  • You want flexibility in how you manage the balance

How We Approach It

Matched to your situation, not a template

We look at how the payment interacts with the rest of your financial picture — reserves, other obligations, and what happens when the interest-only period ends.

The aim is a structure you enter with clear eyes: aware of the benefits during the initial period and prepared for the adjustment that follows.
